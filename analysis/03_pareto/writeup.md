# 03  - Pareto Chart

## Tool
Pareto Chart (80/20 Analysis). Reference: Ch.2 Basic 7 QC Tools. Diagram produced by Team C-2 from survey data.

## Purpose
A Pareto chart is a vertical bar chart combined with a cumulative frequency line, used to rank causes or defect categories by their frequency or impact. The underlying Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule) asserts that roughly 80 percent of effects arise from 20 percent of causes. By sorting bars in descending order and overlaying a cumulative percentage curve, the chart immediately identifies the "vital few" categories that warrant priority attention, as opposed to the "trivial many" that contribute marginally.

## Application in Our Project
The chart was constructed from Q12 of our 150-respondent survey, which asked students to select all pain points they had personally experienced. Respondents could select multiple categories. The seven categories plotted are: long wait (90 respondents), payment issue (71), no night availability (66), route detour (62), empty return seen (59), rude driver (52), and none (4). Team C-2 produced this chart using the cleaned survey data and standard Pareto conventions (bars sorted descending, cumulative line on secondary y-axis, 80% threshold marked).

## Key Findings and Insights
The top two categories, long wait and payment issue, together account for 161 selections, which is 45.2 percent of the total 356 non-"none" responses. Including no night availability and route detour brings the cumulative share above 83 percent. This confirms the Pareto Principle applies: four categories capture more than 80 percent of reported pain. Key implications:
- Long wait time is the single dominant complaint and must be the primary metric for any intervention.
- Payment issues rank second despite cash being the default for most campus transactions, indicating the problem is salient enough that students specifically remember and report it.
- No night availability and route detours, while distinct in cause, both relate to the same structural failure: the service is not demand-responsive.
- Rude driver, while real, is the lowest-ranked pain point and is a downstream symptom of driver frustration rather than a root structural cause.

## How It Informs the Solution
The Pareto chart directly prioritises the app feature set. The booking + 1-minute window feature addresses long wait. The wallet payment feature addresses payment issues. The proximity discovery and scheduling features address night availability and route detour. The chart also signals what not to over-invest in at MVP stage: driver training and courtesy features can be deferred until structural issues are resolved, since they rank last and may self-correct when driver earnings stabilise under the new model.
