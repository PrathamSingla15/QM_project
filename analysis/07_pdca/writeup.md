# 07  - PDCA Cycle

## Tool
PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) Cycle, also called the Deming Cycle or Shewhart Cycle. Reference: Ch.1 TQM Fundamentals.

## Purpose
PDCA is the foundational iterative improvement model in TQM. It prescribes that any improvement initiative must begin with a plan grounded in data, proceed through a controlled do phase (implementation, typically piloted at small scale), pause to check outcomes against the planned targets using objective measurement, and then act by standardising what worked and iterating on what did not. The cycle is explicitly designed to repeat: each rotation improves the baseline for the next. PDCA prevents the common failure mode of implementing a solution without defined success criteria or measurement, which makes it impossible to know whether the intervention worked.

## Application in Our Project
We mapped the E-rickshaw app improvement initiative onto the PDCA cycle. The Plan phase uses the outputs of the Pareto chart, Ishikawa diagram, and 5 Whys to define three measurable KPIs: average student wait time, fare leakage percentage, and empty return trip percentage. It also specifies designing an app MVP and selecting a single pilot zone (rather than a campus-wide rollout) to keep the Do phase controlled. The Do phase limits the pilot to 5 E-ricks and 50 students for 2 weeks, which is large enough to generate statistically meaningful data but small enough to iterate quickly. The Check phase compares all three KPIs against the pre-pilot baseline captured from the survey, and also runs a post-pilot satisfaction survey. The Act phase uses those findings to standardise successful features and update driver Standard Operating Procedures before expanding.

## Key Findings and Insights
Applying PDCA reveals two risks in how the project might otherwise have been executed:
- Without a defined Check phase, the team might have declared success based on anecdotal positive feedback rather than measured KPI improvement. The explicit Check phase prevents this.
- Without a small-scale Do phase, a failed launch on the full campus would be both costly and politically difficult to recover from. The pilot structure makes failure recoverable and learnable.

## How It Informs the Solution
The PDCA structure dictates several concrete deliverables beyond the app itself: a pre-pilot data collection protocol (baseline wait times, cash reconciliation logs), a 2-week pilot run plan, a post-pilot survey instrument, and a driver SOP document to be updated in the Act phase. These are now listed as required deliverables in the project plan. The PDCA cycle also provides the board-level justification for why a phased rollout is the correct approach: it is not caution for its own sake, but a disciplined improvement methodology with a defined expansion trigger.
